How to Find Low-Competition Keywords for Free (2025 Guide)

If your blog is new, the fastest way to get traffic is by targeting low-competition keywords. These are search terms that people search for, but very few websites have written about. Ranking for these keywords is easier, faster, and requires no backlinks.

In this guide, you’ll learn the best free methods to find low-competition keywords using tools like Google, Pinterest, and YouTube.

What Are Low-Competition Keywords?

These are keywords that:

  • Have high search demand
  • Have low competition
  • Are usually longer (3–6 words)
  • Are easy to rank within weeks

Examples:

  • “Make money online without investment”
  • “Simple Pinterest SEO tips”
  • “Monetag setup for beginners”

1. Use Google Autosuggest (Completely Free)

Go to the Google search bar and type your main topic. Google will automatically show keywords people are searching for.

Example: type “affiliate marketing”

You will see suggestions like:

  • affiliate marketing for beginners
  • affiliate marketing without investment
  • affiliate marketing using Pinterest

These are perfect low-competition keywords.

2. Use “People Also Ask” Box

Google’s “People Also Ask” box gives you hundreds of real questions users search for.

Example queries:

  • “How can beginners make money online?”
  • “Which affiliate program is best?”
  • “How does Monetag work?”

Every question can be a new blog article.

3. Use Pinterest Search (Beginners’ Secret Weapon)

Pinterest is an underrated keyword tool. Type any topic into Pinterest search — Pinterest will show keyword suggestions people use daily.

Example: Type “make money online”

You will see:

  • make money online fast
  • make money online ideas
  • make money online without experience
  • side hustles for beginners

If Pinterest shows it → there is demand.

4. Use YouTube Suggestions

YouTube is the second-largest search engine. Type your topic into the search bar and see suggestions.

Example: “SEO tips for beginners”

You will see:

  • SEO tips for new bloggers
  • SEO without a website
  • SEO for Pinterest

These topics have low competition and high traffic potential.

5. Use Free Chrome Extensions

  • Keyword Surfer
  • Ubersuggest (free version)
  • Detailed SEO Extension

These give search volume estimates and similar keywords.

6. Search Competitor Blogs

Find small competitor blogs in your niche and analyze their articles. If they rank with small content, you can do better.

Look at:

  • Titles
  • Headings
  • Article word count
  • Which articles get comments or shares

7. Use Google Trends

Google Trends helps you find trending keywords with rising interest.

Examples:

  • AI tools
  • Crypto news
  • Budget travel
  • Work from home jobs

Write early, rank fast.

8. Use Question-Based Keywords

Keywords that start with:

  • How to…
  • Best ways to…
  • Why does…
  • What is…
  • Should I…

Question keywords have lower competition and rank quickly.

9. Look for Keywords With Weak Competition

Check if the top results are:

  • Forums
  • Pinterest results
  • Quora answers
  • Medium posts

If yes → competition is weak. You can rank!

10. Make a List of 50+ Low-Competition Keywords

Use all methods above and create a list. Then write 1–2 articles daily or weekly. Targeting low-competition keywords is the fastest way to grow a new blog.

Conclusion

You don’t need expensive tools to find keywords. Using Google, Pinterest, YouTube, and simple research, you can find hundreds of low-competition keywords for free. Write consistently, target simple topics, and you’ll see traffic growth within weeks.
